Lege

Number of players: more than 2

Materials:

  • Rings with a diameter of 7cm (made of tin foil)
  • Cord
  • Clothes pegs

Instructions:You can choose to be either on your own or team up in pairs of two, where one teammate has to ride on the back of the other. A cord is placed at head height with all the rings hanging about one meter (three feet) apart attached to it. A starting line is placed about eight meters (24 feet) away from the cord. If not in teams the players have to run at constant speed, with their arm outstretched and their forefinger pointing towards the rings, trying to catch the rings. If in teams the one riding on the others back has to try to catch the ring with their arm outstretched, while the one carrying the other has to run towards the rings. The objective is to catch the ring as many times as possible in ten tries.

Number of players:3-12

Materials:

  • None

Intstruktions:The players have to stand in a circle. A person is chosen to start the game by touching one of the other players with a limb of their choosing e.g. leg, arm or nose. That limb is now stuck to the other person and the person who started has to keep their limb at the place they chose to touch the other player. That is called slurping, and when slurping someone you have to make a loud “sluuurp” noise. The turn is passed on the person who was just slurped, and that person has to slurp someone else. The game ends when someone breaks their slurp and remove one of their limbs from another person.

Number of players:more than 16

Materials:

  • The neckerchiefs of the players

Intstruktions:

Variation 1:
The players have to put the end of their neckerchief into their waistband, and let the other end hang out like a tail. The objective is to steal the others neckerchiefs without having your own taken. If you lose your neckerchief you are out of the game. If you have stolen a neckerchief you must put it in your pocket so that the others can tell which one they have to steal from you.The winner is the player who manages to steal all the other neckerchiefs.

Variation 2:
he players have to put the end of their neckerchief into their waistband, and let the other end hang out like a tail. The objective is to steal the others neckerchiefs without having your own taken. If you lose your tail you keep going trying to get a new one. Every time you steal a tail you put it into your waistband and in that way you have more tails that can be stolen. A timer is set to 5-10 minutes. The winner is whoever has the most tails when time is up.

Number of players: 8

Materials:

  • Langt torv

Intstruktions:The long rope has to be tied together so it makes up a large circle. A person is chosen to be the bull and has to stand in the center of the circle made out of rope. The other players has to stand outside of the circle and lift the rope, so that it does not touch the ground. The bull has to catch one of the players standing outside of the circle. The players trying to avoid the bull is allowed to let go of the rope, but the rope must not touch the ground. The players holding the rope can run around with the rope too, giving the circle different shapes as the game progresses. If the bull manages to catch someone, they have to swap places, so that the person caught becomes the bull. If the rope touches the ground the bull chooses someone to swap places with.

Number of players: more than 15

Materials:

  • None

Intstruktions:Choose two taggers who have to hold hands, making a chain. They have to try to catch the others without letting go of each other. Every time the taggers catches someone, this person has to join the chain which tagged them. When a chain consists of four people, it splits up making two chains with two taggers each. The last person to be tagged wins the game. Hint: The game can be played without the chains breaking up when reaching four people, and this way ending up creating one large chain.

Number of players: more than 8

Materials:

  • None

Intstruktions:Two players are chosen to be the farmers. The rest are lying in a circle, holding on to each other by the crooks of their arms, are the carrots. The farmers have to harvest the carrots by pulling at the carrots’ feet. When a carrot is pulled out of the circle they are out of the game. When a carrot is pulled out of the circle the others have to reassemble the circle. The game ends when the farmers pull the two last carrots apart.

Number of players: more than 16

Materials:

  • None

Intstruktions:The players stand in to two circles having the same number of players. One of the circles has to be inside the other one. The inner circle is “the mountain”, and the outer circle is the “joggers”. One of the “joggers” has to stand in the center of the inner circle, and chooses when to yell “The mountain is burning!”. When this happens the other “joggers” are terrified and starts running clockwise around “the mountain”. The “joggers” are allowed to overtake each other but cannot run anything but clockwise. When the person the in the middle then yells “the fire has been put out”, all the joggers has to stand in front of a “mountain” person. The “jogger” who does not manage to find a “mountain” person to stand in front of swaps places with the “jogger” in the middle. After a couple of turns the “mountain” and the “joggers’” roles change, making the “mountain” people the “joggers” and vice versa.

Number of players: more than 8

Materials:

  • None

Intstruktions:A player has to be the tagger who tags the other players by touching them. When a player is tagged they have to stand still on the spot they were tagged with their legs parted in an A shape. The players who have not been tagged can free a tagged player by crawling through their legs. The game can be bounded by a timer of ten minutes or it can go on until the tagger have tagged all the other players.

Variation 1: The game follow the same rules but every time the the tagger tags someone they count how many they have tagged by yelling the number out loud. When the tagger tags the third person this person becomes the tagger instead.

Variation 2: The game follows the same rules, but the players have to team up in pairs. When a pair is tagged they have to create a archway with their arms and is freed when another pair runs through the archway. To make the game more fun add criteria to the archway e.g. both players have to balance on one leg.

Number of players: 4-10 pairs

Materials:

  • Blindfolds (e.g. neckerchiefs)

Intstruktions:The players have to team up in pairs. One of the players has to be driver and the other one has to be the bumper car. One of the pairs are chosen to be the killer bumper car. All the pairs have to agree on some words that have to indicate “forward”, “reverse”, “left”, “right” and “stop”, as they are not allowed to use those words. All the players who are bumper cars have to be blindfolded. A course is created by the drivers who have to stand in a circle with all the bumper cars inside it. The bumper cars has to listen to their driver who will tell them where to go by using the words the team agreed upon. The killer bumper car has to try to catch the other bumper cars, but is only allowed to move in the directions they are told by their driver - just like the other cars. When a car is hit by the killer bumper car it stops working and has to stand still on the spot they were hit. The last bumper car to be hit by the killer bumper car has to be the killer bumper car in the next round. The drivers and the bumper cars swap roles every new round. Hint: For new players to learn the game make a test round. If necessary mark the killer bumper car somehow, so the drivers knows who is the killer bumper car.

Number of players: 8-12

Materials:

  • None

Intstruktions:A player is chosen to be the starter dragon who wants to catch the princess. Another player is chosen to be the starter princess who tries to avoid the dragon. The rest of the players have to team up in pairs, and stand in a circle. The pairs have to hold on to each other by the crooks of their arms. The princess tries to flee from the dragon. If the princess is caught by the dragon the roles swaps, and the former dragon is now the princess and the former princess is now the dragon. If the princess gets tired of fleeing from the dragon she can grab onto one the players who stands in a pair. The person in the pair who is not holding on to the princess is now the new dragon, and the former dragon becomes the princess. The game ends after a specific time, that the players agree upon before starting. It is important that the players who are either the dragon or the princess enter into the spirit of their role. The dragon has to use their arms as wings and roar, and the princess has to scream while running. Otherwise it is not possible to tell the dragon and the princess apart. Hint: It is possible to have two dragons and princesses in play at the same time, but it easily gets confusing.

Number of players: 8-12

Materials:

  • None

Intstruktions:All the players have to stand in a line with their hands on the shoulders of the player in front of them. The person who is at the front of the line is the head of the dragon, and the person who is at the back of the line is the tail. The head of the dragon has to try to catch the tail without the line, which is the dragon, breaking apart. When the head catches the tail, the head has to go to the back of the line becoming the tail. Now the new head in the front has to catch the new tail. The game goes on until the players do not want to play anymore.
